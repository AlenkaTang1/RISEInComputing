Program Overview

The RISE Scholars Program is designed to support and empower undergraduate computing students at Georgia State University, with a special focus on enhancing the experiences of Black women in computing. Through mentorship, academic support, and professional development opportunities, we aim to create a more inclusive and equitable environment in computer science education.

Mentorship

Connect with experienced faculty members and industry professionals who will guide you through your academic journey and career development.

Financial Support

Access to scholarships, stipends, and funding opportunities to support your education and research activities.

Professional Development

Participate in workshops, conferences, and networking events to build your professional skills and industry connections.

Research Opportunities

Engage in meaningful research projects under the guidance of faculty mentors and gain valuable research experience.

How to Apply

We welcome applications from undergraduate students who are passionate about computing and committed to academic excellence.

Requirements:

  • Currently enrolled as an undergraduate student at Georgia State University
  • Pursuing a degree in Computer Science or related field
  • Minimum GPA of 3.0
  • Demonstrated interest in computing and technology
  • Commitment to diversity and inclusion in computing
